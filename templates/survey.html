This survey is for Dyslexia

This survey is specially designed for parents and teachers to test if their child/student is dyslexic or not. This survey is based on your keen observation towards your child/student.
Question 1

Does this student seem to be developing more slowly than other children of same age?




Question 2

Does this student have difficulty in communicating or socialising with others?




Question 3

Does this student have problems in remembering things?




Question 4

Do you notice repetitive gestures like spinning on a certain spot, enjoy a particular lifestyle and way of object arrangements which can otherwise cause anxiety, in this student?




Question 5

Does this student react to external changes in unexpected manner? For example, those who are non-chalant (unconcerned/disinterested) towards parents’ yelling and unresponsive to stimuli such as stinging, hotness and coldness.




Question 6

Is this student comparatively slow in reading or writing?




Question 7

Does this student have difficulty with written expression (e.g., problems with grammar, punctuation or organization) even after being taught several times?




Question 8

Does this child lack sense of direction?




Question 9

Does this student reverse letters or symbols while writing? For example, writing “q” instead “p” or b/d, u/v, w/m, frequently?




Question 10

Does this student repeat words or parts of words? For example, when asked ‘Do you want to drink water’ the child will reply “You want to drink water”




Question 11

Does this student speak in short, fragmented phrases?




Question 12

Does your child worry for sequences, facts and information that were not taught before?




Question 13

Does this student say words in the wrong order, for example saying “blue sky is” instead of ‘sky is blue’?




Question 14

Does this student struggle in the use of words, for example repeating words again and again in a sentence or unable to use appropriate words?




Question 15

Does this student have difficulty in imitating speech sounds, for example a child is asked to repeat the word “boat” but he fails to repeat in the desired manner?




Question 16

Does this student speak unclearly so that the sounds run into one another?




Question 17

Does this student make a lot of repeated syntax (grammatical) or semantics (structural) mistakes? For example, repeatedly writing ‘he push him’ instead of ‘he pushes/pushed him’.




Question 18

In comparison to other students, does this student have inability to connect actions with consequences? For example, no understanding of meaning of silent gestures (Shh...) or keeping the finger on the mouth to stop talking.




Question 19

Is this student not able to express emotions and needs?




Question 20

Do you think this student has improper eye contact?
