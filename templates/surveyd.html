This survey is for Dyscalculia

This survey is specially designed for parents and teachers to test if their child/student is dyscalculic or not.
Question 1

Does your child have difficulty with basic math concepts such as counting, addition, subtraction, and multiplication?





Question 2

Does your child have difficulty remembering number facts or math formulas?





Question 3

Does your child have difficulty understanding and applying math concepts to solve problems?





Question 4

Does your child struggle to recognize patterns and relationships between numbers?





Question 5

Does your child have difficulty telling time on an analog clock?





Question 6

Does your child have difficulty estimating quantities and measurements?





Question 7

Does your child have difficulty with spatial reasoning skills, such as understanding and manipulating shapes and angles?





Question 8

Does your child have difficulty with money concepts, such as counting coins and making change?





Question 9

Does your child have difficulty with sequencing and organizing information, such as steps in a math problem or in a sequence of events?





Question 10

Does your child show anxiety or avoidance behaviors when faced with math-related tasks?





Question 11

Does you child show difficulty in navigating or mentally estimating the measurement of a “turning” the map to identify the current direction?





Question 12

Is your child unusually intense and/or persistent-refuses to progress when time is up and becomes upset when pressurized to go to next question in exams?





Question 13

Does your child specifically have difficulty in single digit addition and multiplication like (3+2 and 3*2)?





Question 14

Does your child know the correct sequence of solution steps for multistep calculating tasks?





Question 15

Does your child intentionally sabotage scores by answering incorrectly?





Question 16

Can your child visualize big picture and identify key points?





Question 17

Does your child face problems in differentiating between left and right and face trouble using a calculator?





Question 18

Does your child show difficulty in reading or interpreting graphs?





Question 19

Does your child show difficulty in visual representation of mathematics concepts?





Question 20

Does your child have difficulty connecting between a number (e.g., 2) and the quantity it represents (e.g., 2 apples)?
