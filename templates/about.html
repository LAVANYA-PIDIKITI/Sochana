Dyslexia

Dyslexia is a common learning difficulty that affects the ability to read and write accurately and fluently. It is a neurological disorder that can cause difficulty with phonological processing, phonemic awareness, and working memory.
Dyslexia can impact a person's academic performance and overall confidence. However, with the right support and interventions, individuals with dyslexia can thrive and achieve their goals.

Dyscalculia

Dyscalculia is a learning disability that affects a person's ability to understand and work with numbers. Individuals with dyscalculia may have difficulty performing simple arithmetic, reading analog clocks, and measuring time.
It is estimated that dyscalculia affects about 5-7% of the population. Dyscalculia can be diagnosed and managed with appropriate interventions and accommodations.

Autism

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), is a complex neurodevelopmental disorder that affects communication, social interaction, and behavior.
Symptoms of autism can vary widely, but may include difficulties in verbal and nonverbal communication, difficulty in social interactions, and repetitive behaviors or interests.
Autism is a lifelong condition, but early intervention can help improve outcomes and quality of life for individuals with autism.
